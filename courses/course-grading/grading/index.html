Grading

Details on how proficiency grading relates to the specific learning tasks are provided here. All items submitted use the same rubric, though some grades are not possible for certain tasks (e.g., Learning Journals cannot earn an “Excellent” or “Not Yet”).

  • Does the work demonstrate thorough understanding of the concepts? Does the work meet the expectations outlined in the assignment?
    • Yes. Is the work complete and well communicated?
      • Yes; Excellent: The work meets or exceeds the expectations of the assignment. Communication is clear and complete. Mastery of the concepts is evident. There are not any major errors. This work could be used as an exemplary classroom example.
      • No; Satisfactory: Understanding of the concepts is evident through correct work and clear, audience-appropriate explanations. Some revision or expansion is needed, but no major gaps or errors are present. No additional instruction on the concepts is needed.
    • No. Is there evidence of partial understanding?
      • Yes; Not Yet: Partial understanding of the concepts is evident, but there are major gaps that remain. Needs further work, more review, and/or improved explanations.
      • No; Incomplete: Not enough information is present in the work to determine whether there is understanding of the concepts. The work is fragmentary or contains major omissions. Or, there are too many issues to justify correcting each one.

This ESNI rubric is based on the EMRN rubric from Dr. Robert Talbert: http://bit.ly/talbert_emrn

Learning Activities

Preparations and Learning Journals will be graded either Satisfactory or Incomplete.

  • A Satisfactory is given if the work is turned in on time and every item has a response that represents a good-faith effort to be complete.
  • An Incomplete will be given if the work has missing responses (even if by accident), work that includes responses like, “I don’t know,” or shows insufficient effort to give a good-faith response and work that is late (even if by accident).

Correctness of a submission is not used to determine your mark.

Learning Target Checks

There are twenty Learning Targets in this course, of which ten are designated as Core targets due to their nature in statistical computing and the other ten are designated as Supplemental targets. There are two levels of achievement for a Learning Target: Competent and Proficient. You will have numerous opportunities to demonstrate your level of understanding on the Learning Targets throughout the semester and every time they will be graded either Satisfactory, Not Yet, or Incomplete.

  • To have a Learning Target rated as Competent, you must earn one Satisfactory
  • To have a Learning Target rated as Proficient, you must earn two Satisfactory

You can earn a Satisfactory on a Learning Target in four different ways:

  1. Complete the Learning Target on a Learning Target Check that meets the criteria for acceptable quality.
  2. Schedule an oral assessment through a video conference where I will provide you with a new version of a Learning Target Check type problem and you work through the problem live at an acceptable level.
  3. Create a video of yourself working out the solution to an Learning Target Check type problem, that I will create for you, and submit the video. Your video must adhere to standards for video creation (e.g., video must have clear sound and keep your face, voice, work in the frame at all times). You will also be expected to have a follow-up video conference with me to discuss the solution and answer additional related questions.
  4. Use work on a Project and schedule a video conference with me to explain how your work on the Project demonstrates proficiency of the Learning Target and answer additional related questions.

No more than one Satisfactory can be earned through options 2-4. That is, to have a Learning Target rated as Proficient, you must earn at least one Satisfactory through an Learning Target Check.

Projects

Work on Projects will earn one of four designations: Excellent, Satisfactory, Not Yet, or Incomplete. Whenever you submit a Project, you will be provided with actionable feedback on what was done well along with what needs to be improved. Specific details on how these marks are assigned for each Project will be provided to you and discussed prior to the Projects being made available.

A grade of “F” is given if not all the requirements for a “D” are met.

Once your base grade has been determined, a plus/minus distinction may be added to your base grade according to these guidelines:
plus (+) A “plus” may be added to your base grade if all the requirements for a base grade are satisfied, you complete at least 21 Preparations, and you complete at least 36 Learning Journals.
minus (-) A “minus” may be added to your base grade for any of the following cases:
1. All requirements for a base grade are satisfied except one that is no more than two levels below the others.
2. You do not earn a Satisfactory+ on the Team Project;
3. You complete less than 16 Preparations; OR
4. You complete less than 14 Learning Journals.

Note that if the deficient area is more than two levels lower or you have three or four “minus” cases, you will either earn a “minus” or earn a full letter grade lower, at your instructor’s discretion;

Also note that an “A+” or a “D-” cannot be awarded since these grades do not exist at GVSU.
